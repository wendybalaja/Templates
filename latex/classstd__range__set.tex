\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
\subsubsection*{template$<$typename T, typename C = comp$<$\+T$>$, typename I = increment$<$\+T$>$$>$\newline
class std\+\_\+range\+\_\+set$<$ T, C, I $>$}

As implemented in the standard library, sets contain individual elements, not ranges. (There are range insert and erase operators, but (a) they use iterators, (b) they take time proportional to the number of elements in the range, and (c) they require, for deletion, that the endpoints of the range actually be in the set. An \mbox{\hyperlink{classstd__range__set}{std\+\_\+range\+\_\+set}}, as defined here, avoids shortcomings (a) and (c), but not (b). Your bin\+\_\+search\+\_\+range\+\_\+set should avoid (b), though it will have slow insert and remove operations. A tree\+\_\+range\+\_\+set (search tree -- extra credit) would have amortized log-\/time insert and remove for individual elements {\itshape and} ranges.
